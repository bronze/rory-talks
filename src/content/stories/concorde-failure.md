title: "Why Concorde Didn't Really Work"
description: "Concorde failed on two counts: it flew west-to-east, the direction where you should be asleep rather than awake in a pressurised jet, and no individual passenger flew the route frequently enough for the time saving to change their life. Rory calculated that Concorde's heaviest-ever user — who was on both its first and last flights — saved only about five minutes a day over his working life; he could have achieved the same by moving slightly closer to his office. The story illustrates that time savings matter only when they are large enough, frequent enough, and aligned with the right direction of travel to be genuinely life-altering."
