title: "Google and Goal Dilution"
description: "While every other search engine tried to be a portal — offering weather, sports scores, and news alongside search — Google did only one thing, and people assumed it must be very good at that one thing. Rory links this to the 'goal dilution' effect described by Ayelet Fishbach: a product that does one thing is perceived as better at it than a product that does that thing plus something else, illustrated by the way shoppers instinctively distrust combined TV and DVD players."
