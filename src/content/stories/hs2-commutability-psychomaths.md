title: "HS2 and the Commutability Psychomaths"
description: "Standard transport planning treats 100 people saving 40 minutes 10 times a year as identical to 10 people saving 40 minutes 100 times a year — the aggregate time saving is the same. But psychologically they are completely different: the frequent saving is a life-changing event that makes a previously uncommutable place commutable, while the infrequent saving is a mild convenience. Rory argues High Speed 2 spends billions on mild convenience for millions when it could instead transform the lives of a smaller number of people far more profoundly."
