title: "Tennis Scoring Creates Drama"
description: "Richard Thomasson observed that all the excitement of tennis comes from its scoring system — compartmentalised sets, holding and breaking serve — and that if you scored tennis like basketball it would be unwatchable. A basketball critic made the same point in reverse: just start at 100 points each and play the last four minutes. Illustrates how narrative structure, not raw events, creates perceived value and excitement."
