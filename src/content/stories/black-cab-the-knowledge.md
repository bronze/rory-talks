title: "The Black Cab Knowledge as a Trust Signal"
description: "London black cab drivers must pass 'The Knowledge' — an extraordinarily demanding test requiring one to two years of memorising every street in London, typically studied on a moped with a clipboard. Now that satnavs exist, this may seem pointlessly expensive. But Rory argues The Knowledge served a deeper function: the sheer difficulty of acquiring it was a sunk-cost commitment device that reliably screened out dishonest drivers, in the same way medieval guilds used stringent entry conditions to guarantee honesty."
