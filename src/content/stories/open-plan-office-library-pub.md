title: "The Open-Plan Office: Neither Library Nor Pub"
description: "The open-plan office was a mistake because it optimised for the average: it is neither quiet enough for solitary concentration nor social enough for genuine conversation. Rory argues the future of the office is half library, half pub — embracing both extremes rather than averaging them into something that does neither job well. This is his illustration of the general principle that when two good ideas contradict each other, the average is usually worse than either extreme."
