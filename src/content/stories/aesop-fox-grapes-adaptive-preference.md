description: "Rory invokes the fable of the fox who declares the unreachable grapes must be sour as an illustration of adaptive preference formation: when we cannot have A, we construct a narrative in which we prefer B anyway, minimising regret. He argues that good choice architecture should deliberately build in the potential for this reframing — so that the person who doesn't get the seat on a train can still tell themselves a story about why standing was actually better."
