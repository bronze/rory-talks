title: "The Sabbath vs. the 35-Hour Working Week"
description: "France addressed overwork with the 35-hour working time directive — a precise, rational, quantitative limit that is nearly impossible to monitor or enforce. Three thousand years earlier, the Sabbath solved the same problem with a binary rule everyone observes simultaneously: everyone stops on the same day. The Sabbath is less mathematically exact but far more psychologically robust because it is crunchy, binary, and socially self-reinforcing."
