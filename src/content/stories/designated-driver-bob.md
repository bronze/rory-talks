description: "The phrase \"designated driver\" was invented by marketers, and simply by naming the role it became possible for someone to sit in a pub drinking Coke without awkwardness — the name explained the behaviour. In the Netherlands, a separate campaign gave the role the name \"Bob,\" so the question \"who's going to be Bob tonight?\" created an easy, socially shareable heuristic for ensuring a sober driver. Naming a behaviour makes it both cognitively easy and socially contagious."
