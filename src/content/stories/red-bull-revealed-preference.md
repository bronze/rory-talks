description: "Nobody in a focus group would ever ask for an expensive drink in a small can that tastes unpleasant — yet Red Bull became a global phenomenon. Rory uses it as a canonical example of 'revealed preference': a product that consumers would have rationally rejected in advance but love in practice. It illustrates why pre-rationalisation from market research systematically screens out the most valuable innovations."
