## Key Takeaways

- All data comes from the past, yet the future is annoyingly different — demanding evidential proof before acting systematically excludes the most valuable insights, as Nokia discovered when it ignored an anthropologist's warning about smartphones.
- Separate investigative value from evidential value: in a police inquiry you'd never restrict information-gathering to only what could stand up in court, yet that is precisely what data-driven organisations demand of strategy.
- Creativity in strategy comes from three places: resolving a trade-off you hate in its entirety, spotting an anomaly and asking what model of the world would put it on the regression line, or importing an analogy from elsewhere.
- Statistical focus on the mean drives attention away from anomalies — but anomalies are often the only signal that the future has already arrived in a small corner of the world.
- The most useful strategic question is "What would have to be true for this to happen?" — it turns an implausible idea into a checklist of conditions that can be tested or created.
