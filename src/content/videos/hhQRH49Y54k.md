## Key Takeaways

- Behavioural economics is simply proper economics — it studies what people actually do, rather than deriving elegant mathematical formulas for what imaginary rational agents should do.
- Being logical is a strategic liability: in military strategy and business alike, complete logical consistency makes you predictable, and predictability makes you exploitable.
- Psychological moonshots are more achievable than technological ones: making a train journey ten times more enjoyable is far easier than making it ten times faster, yet organisations almost always pursue the engineering path.
- R&D and marketing are really the same activity approached from opposite ends — finding what people want and making it, or making something and finding people who want it, with equal economic value either way.
- Finance departments hate marketing because they cannot model the compounding, fat-tailed, often delayed returns from brand-building — and what cannot be modelled is treated as if it does not exist.
- The "explore-exploit trade-off" that governs bee foraging also governs healthy businesses: devoting some resources to random exploration, not just exploiting what already works, is what prevents catastrophic over-optimisation.
