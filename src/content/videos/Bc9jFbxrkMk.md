## Key Takeaways

- The relationship between speed and time saved is non-linear: going faster when you're already slow saves a lot, but going faster when you're already fast saves almost nothing — yet we systematically ignore this and keep accelerating.
- We have allowed the urgent to drown out the important, like the account man who wishes back the copywriter and art director to make a meeting — optimising for speed at the cost of quality and value.
- Presenting the same information differently produces completely different decisions; reframing time and distance reveals that very high-speed rail saves almost no time at distances where you'd be better off flying.
- All rail ticketing apps assume you're in a hurry — but many travellers would trade time for money or experience, and no one is designing for that preference.
- Our perception of time is deeply subjective and easily manipulated; the same objective journey feels radically different depending on how information about it is presented.
