## Key Takeaways

- Choose industries that get better as you age, not worse: sports, modelling and acting typically peak in your 20s, while knowledge-intensive fields compound over decades — career choice is partly a question of how your value trajectory changes over time.
- Capital has been "kicking the shit out of labour" for 50 years: soaring property prices and tuition costs have absorbed the gains of rising productivity, leaving younger generations less wealthy despite working harder than their parents.
- The real divide in modern economies is between rich and non-rich, not left and right; consumer goods companies paradoxically never campaign for the more equitable wealth distribution that would grow their markets.
- Winner-takes-all dynamics are extremely harsh in sport and creative industries: the few who succeed are highly visible while the vast majority who fail are invisible, creating a systematic bias in how we perceive the odds.
- The tech industry's tendency to automate human interaction destroys unquantifiable but significant value — particularly the relational, emotional, and social functions that make services genuinely useful.
- Brands built on psychological value (like Apple's ecosystem lock-in or luxury goods' painful pricing) create moats that are often more durable than those built on engineering advantage alone.
