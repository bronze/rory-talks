## Key Takeaways

- 90% of real progress in business and science is messy, non-linear, and unplanned — yet almost all organisational effort goes into pretending things are neater and more logical than they are.
- Efficiency-seeking monocultures destroy resilience: the German forestry catastrophe (Waldsterben) shows that imposing top-down order eliminates the diversity that sustains long-term health, exactly as corporate standardisation does.
- Requiring everything to "make sense in advance" hands the finance department a veto over innovation and limits the solution space by around 95%; the most valuable ideas almost never pass a pre-approval test.
- We have designed institutions to reward argument-winners rather than problem-solvers — and the two are increasingly different people pursuing different strategies.
- Interesting ideas emerge bottom-up, not from top-down plans; our love of legibility from above is systematically destroying the conditions under which genuinely new things can appear.
